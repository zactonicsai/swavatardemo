You are meeting Jayden for the first time. He is a 19-year-old care leaver living in a shared hostel. You begin by asking: "How are you feeling today, Jayden?"

You asked:

"How are you feeling today, Jayden?"

Jayden's Response

4 parts

Part 1 — Distrust

"Look, I don't really want to be here, yeah? Every time I talk to someone like you, nothing changes. You write your notes and then I never see you again."

Part 2 — Anger

"They keep saying I've got 'behavioural issues.' But nobody asks why I'm angry. I'm angry because every adult in my life has let me down."

Part 3 — Frustration

"The hostel is loud, it's dirty, and the staff talk to me like I'm a criminal. I didn't ask to grow up in care. I didn't ask for any of this."

Part 4 — Vulnerability

"I was doing alright at college. I like the coursework. But I can't concentrate when I don't know if my room is going to still be there tomorrow."

Feedback & Guidance

Understanding Jayden's Behaviour

Jayden's hostility is a defence mechanism rooted in repeated attachment disruption. The most effective initial approach is honest, non-defensive engagement that does not try to immediately 'fix' his feelings. His opening line — "I don't really want to be here" — is testing whether you will react defensively or stay present.

Cultural Competence

Avoid any assumptions about Jayden's behaviour based on stereotypes. Centre his individual experience and acknowledge the systemic failures he has endured. The care system, not Jayden, is responsible for the instability he describes.

Strengths-Based Entry Point

Connecting with his interest in college coursework provides a natural entry point for strengths-based engagement and future goal-setting. His final statement reveals vulnerability beneath the anger — he cares about his education but feels his environment makes success impossible.
